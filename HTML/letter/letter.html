Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my host-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

Polynesian chicken dance

A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock or be "fruiful".

Icelandic brownian shuffle

Before the Icelander developed fire as a mean of getting warm, they used to pratice this dance, which involver huddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffling their bodies around in imperceptably tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired moderm styles such as Twerking

Arctic robot dance

An interesting example of historic misinformation. English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discovered a new style characterised by "robotic", stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that thay were just moving like this because they were really cold.
